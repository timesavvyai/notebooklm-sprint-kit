The Prompt Stack

8 Prompts That Build Your Offer From Scratch

Run these in order. Each one builds on the last. Copy, paste, and edit your answers in the fill-in boxes below each prompt.

Prompt 1: Find the Money

This is your foundation. Most people skip straight to "what should I make?" and get stuck. This forces the AI to think like a buyer — not a content creator.

Prompt 1 NotebookLM
"Looking across all of the content I've uploaded, identify the most specific, urgent, and monetizable problem being solved. Don't give me vague themes or categories. Give me clear, real-world problems that people actively struggle with and would pay to fix. Rank your top 3 findings by: (1) urgency for the person experiencing it, and (2) speed to tangible results. For each one, explain why it ranks there."
Why this prompt works
You're forcing NotebookLM to think commercially — not academically. Vague summaries are useless. This extracts the specific pain people will open their wallets for.

Prompt 2: Extract the Transformation

People don't buy information. They buy the bridge from where they are to where they want to be. This prompt pins that bridge down.

Prompt 2 NotebookLM
"For the top problem you identified — define the transformation in precise detail. What does someone's reality look like BEFORE they solve this problem (what are they feeling, doing, struggling with daily)? What does their reality look like AFTER they have the solution (what's different, what's easier, what's possible now)? Make both sides as vivid and specific as possible."
Why this prompt works
The Before/After contrast is the core of every sale. If you can't describe both sides with specificity, you don't have a sellable offer — yet. This prompt builds that clarity from YOUR content.

Prompt 3: Build the Offer

Now you take that transformation and turn it into a product. Not "my course" — an actual structured thing someone can buy.

Prompt 3 ChatGPT Claude
"I help [WHO] go from [BEFORE state] to [AFTER state]. I want to turn this into a simple, sellable offer. Give me: (1) 5 compelling title options using the formula: Result + Speed + Who It's For, (2) a one-sentence promise statement that captures the core outcome, (3) 4–5 specific outcomes they walk away with, and (4) 3 different format options (workshop, mini-course, sprint kit, etc.) that would work for this. For each format, tell me the pros and cons."
Why this prompt works
You're giving the AI your exact transformation language (from Prompt 2) so it builds from YOUR reality — not generic output. The format options help you pick the path of least resistance to launch.

Prompt 4: Make It Irresistible

Same offer. Different angle. This prompt finds the positioning that makes people feel like they'd be crazy not to buy.

Prompt 4 ChatGPT Claude
"Here's my offer: [paste your title + promise statement]. Give me 5 completely different positioning angles for this — each one targeting a different emotional driver (fear of losing time, desire for status, frustration with the current situation, hope for ease, urgency around a deadline or cost of inaction). For each angle, write a 2–3 sentence hook that I could use as an opening line for a post, email, or video."
Why this prompt works
Most people promote their offer from one angle, to one emotion, forever. Your buyer has multiple reasons to say yes — this finds them all. Pick the one that lands hardest for your audience.

Prompt 5: Structure the Content

Now you know what you're selling. This prompt builds the actual teaching flow — what you'll walk them through inside the offer.

Prompt 5 NotebookLM ChatGPT
"Based on the transformation from [BEFORE] to [AFTER], create a simple, logical step-by-step structure I can use to teach this. Each step should: (1) have a clear, action-oriented name, (2) explain what the person does in that step, and (3) explain what shifts or becomes possible after completing it. Keep it to 4–6 steps max. The goal is that someone could follow this structure and get the promised result — not feel like they're in a college course."
Why this prompt works
NotebookLM can pull the structure from your actual content — it's probably already in there. Or let ChatGPT build it from your transformation. Either way: simple frameworks sell. Complicated ones don't.

Prompt 6: Kill the Objections

The objections are already in their head before they even see your offer. This prompt names them and neutralizes them so your sales message does the work.

Prompt 6 Claude ChatGPT
"My offer is: [title + one-line description]. My target buyer is [who]. List the top 6 reasons this person would hesitate, talk themselves out of buying, or say 'not right now.' For EACH objection: (1) write the exact internal thought they'd have, (2) explain the real fear or belief underneath that thought, and (3) give me a 1–2 sentence response I can build into my sales copy that addresses it without sounding defensive."
Why this prompt works
Claude is particularly sharp at this — it goes beneath the surface objection to the real one. Knowing what your buyer is secretly thinking is the difference between copy that converts and copy that gets politely ignored.

Prompt 7: Write the Sales Message

This becomes your landing page intro, your email body, your DM script. One prompt, reused everywhere.

Prompt 7 ChatGPT Claude
"Write a short, punchy sales message for my offer. Here's what you need to know: [Offer name]. [Who it's for]. [Before state — what they're dealing with]. [After state — what changes]. [Top objection — and my response to it]. [Price and format]. Write this in a tone that is: direct, confident, conversational, a little bold — not salesy or hype-y. Open with a hook that calls out the exact frustration. End with a clear, simple CTA. Under 250 words."
Why this prompt works
You're feeding the AI your actual transformation language, your audience's real objections, and a clear tone brief — so instead of generic copy, you get something that actually sounds like you and speaks to your buyer.

Prompt 8: Validate the Demand

Before you spend hours building — take 10 minutes to confirm people actually want this. This prompt is your reality check.

Prompt 8 Gemini ChatGPT
"I'm about to launch an offer called [title] for [who]. Before I build it, help me validate demand. Search for evidence that people are actively looking for solutions to this problem. What are they Googling? What questions are they asking in communities, subreddits, or forums? What language do they use when they describe this problem themselves? Are there competitors solving this and what are their buyers saying in reviews or comments? Give me the real words buyers use — not marketing language."
Why this prompt works
Gemini has live web access — use that. The buyer language it finds is pure gold for your copy. If people are searching for it and talking about it, you have demand. If not, pivot now before you build. This 10 minutes can save you weeks.
